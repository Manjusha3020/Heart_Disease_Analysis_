About the project

Why this dataset, and why Tableau

Heart disease is one of the leading causes of death worldwide, and its reach keeps growing with modern lifestyle changes, poor diet, and inactivity. Prevention and early detection still make the biggest difference — but spotting the right patterns in patient demographics, medical history, and lifestyle data takes the right tools.

This project uses Tableau to turn a raw heart disease dataset into a dashboard and a guided story, surfacing the risk factors that matter most and the correlations that support better decisions — for clinicians, policymakers, and individuals alike. This site embeds that dashboard and story in a Flask front end so anyone can explore them in a browser.

Who this helps

Three people, three ways of reading the same data

The dashboard is built to answer different questions depending on who's looking at it.

Cardiologist

Dr. Sharma

A senior cardiologist at a metropolitan hospital, trying to pin down which lifestyle factors drive rising heart disease among middle-aged patients. She segments by age, BMI, cholesterol, and smoking to identify high-risk groups and design targeted campaigns — weight management, smoking cessation, and more.

Health policy

Ramesh

Works with a government health department, shaping preventive policy. He compares heart disease prevalence across rural and urban populations and studies how sedentary lifestyle indicators correlate with disease rates — informing workplace fitness programs, tobacco regulation, and healthy-food subsidies.

Patient

Anita, 45

Has a family history of heart disease and wants to track her own risk. A simplified dashboard lets her compare her cholesterol, blood pressure, and lifestyle habits against healthy benchmarks, with clear next steps — more activity, less fat — she can act on.
